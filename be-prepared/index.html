Be flood aware

Low-lying areas of the Upper Nehalem Valley have flooded many times before and will flood again. Reminding family and friends at the beginning of flood season, or letting your new neighbor know about flood hazards, is the easiest way to be prepared as a community. Many new residents to the area have found themselves on the wrong side of flood waters by simply not being aware of the risk of flooding in the Upper Nehalem Valley. Even life long residents have been taken by surprise by high water events. Ignorance and complacency are the biggest threats to life and property from flooding.

Use the Upper Nehalem Flood Map to view flood hazard areas.

Being aware of flood risks is of no use if you are not informed when a flood event is forecasted and/or inevitable. Here are several ways you can be alerted to potential flood events.

  • Sign up for the Columbia Alert Network to receive notifications when a flood is forecasted.
  • Many weather apps have the ability to send notifications when several weather advisories and warnings are issued. Check that notifications are enabled.
  • Join the Upper Nehalem Flood Facebook page or follow us on Twitter.

Protect your life

  • Floodwaters rise quickly and move erratically. Don't enter floodwaters on foot, in a vehicle or boat.
  • Do not risk your life attempting to provide assistance to others not in immediate danger. An emergency management and response plan is in place. First responders and the National Guard are trained and prepared to assist distressed persons.
  • Have a household flood plan and review it with your family every year. Check out our Flood Planning page for more information.
  • Floodwaters can be polluted with biological and chemical contaminants. Minimize contact with floodwaters and sediments left after a flood.
